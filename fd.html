IMPROVING DRIBBLING AND BALL HANDLING

The primary fundamentals to moving the ball around and getting to your goal is dribbling. Dribbling the ball involves exerting a force from your fingers to push the ball down to make it bounce back up. There are many tricks and variations, as opponents will attempt to stop and block you. If you're lucky and explosive/swift enough, you can fake your opponent out and make them fall over. Unfortunately, injuries are common, such as broken ankles. Certain precautions are recommended, such as dynamic stretching and ankle protectors.

Common tricks include:
    1. The crossover
    2. Between the legs
    3. Spin-move
    4. Behind the back
The primary goal in dominating the floor is to create space to shoot. There are many ways for creating "space," such as dribbling and faking, stealing the ball for a fast break, and passing/setting screens.
